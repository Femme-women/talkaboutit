I got diagnosed with breast cancer

Being diagnosed with breast cancer was a devastating and life-altering experience. It was a huge shock that brought a flood of emotions, including fear, uncertainty, and grief. The thought of the potential changes to my physical and emotional well-being was overwhelming. However, I knew that breast cancer is a treatable disease, and with the support of loved ones and access to the best medical care, I was determined to fight it. The journey ahead was daunting, but I knew that I had to stay positive and take each day as it comes. With a strong support system and a positive attitude, I was ready to face the challenges of treatment and recovery.

What endometriosis feels like

Having endometriosis is a constant struggle with pain and discomfort. Every month, the pain intensifies, making it difficult to move or even get out of bed. It feels like a sharp, shooting pain that radiates throughout my lower abdomen, back, and legs, accompanied by headaches, nausea, and exhaustion. I often have to plan my life around my menstrual cycle, which is unpredictable and lasts for more than a week. Even after my period ends, I may still experience pain, making it hard to focus on work or daily activities. Endometriosis has also taken a toll on my mental health, causing anxiety and depression. It's a difficult condition to live with, but I am determined to manage my symptoms and not let it define my life.
