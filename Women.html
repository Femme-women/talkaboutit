Women's health refers to the branch of medicine that focuses on the treatment and diagnosis of diseases and conditions that affect a woman's physical and emotional well-being. Women have a unique set of health care challenges and are at higher risk of developing certain conditions and diseases than men.








Major Women's Health Issues


Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is a condition in which the breast's cells proliferate out of control. Breast cancer comes in several forms. Which breast cells develop into cancer determine the type of breast cancer.

Endometriosis

A condition where tissue that normally lines the uterus starts to protrude outside of it. The tissue that is associated with endometriosis might be located on the ovaries, fallopian tubes, or intestines.

Infertility

Lose of ability to get pregnant, despite having planned, unprotected sex.  The exact cause is unknown, however it may involve issues with ovulation and low levels of several hormones.

Ovarian and Cervical Cancer

The tissues of the cervix, which connects the uterus and vagina, are where cervical cancer develops. Ovarian cancer forms in the tissues of the cervix, the organ connecting the uterus and vagina

Mental Health

Our emotional, psychological, and social well-being are all parts of our mental health. It influences our thoughts, emotions, and behaviours. Additionally, it influences how we respond to stress, interact with others, and make good decisions. It's crucial at every stage of life.

PCOS

An endocrine condition that results in enlarged ovaries with tiny cysts on the margins. Although the exact aetiology of polycystic ovary syndrome is unknown, it may be a result of both hereditary and environmental factors. Caused when an excess amount of androgen is produced.

PMDD

A severe, occasionally incapacitating progression of premenstrual syndrome (PMS). Premenstrual dysphoric disorder (PMDD) is a severe version of premenstrual syndrome (PMS), which includes behavioural and physical symptoms.

STDs

Infections that are spread from one person to another through sexual contact are known as sexually transmitted illnesses (STIs). Blood, semen, vaginal fluid, and other bodily fluids can carry the bacteria, viruses, or parasites that cause stds. There are multiple types of STDs.

IMPORTANT: Do not take this as professional medical advice, please visit a medical professional in case of any peculiarity noticed in regards to health.
